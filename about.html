About

A research platform for
decentralized construction

Loom Robotics is a research platform exploring decentralized robotic construction systems. Our work centers on LEGOSwarm: a team of autonomous mobile robots that collaboratively assemble structures with local autonomy, minimal communication, and robustness to partial system failure.

Research philosophy

LEGOSwarm emphasizes decentralized execution, local autonomy, minimal communication, stigmergic coordination, and robustness to partial system failure. The supervisory Foreman parses a digital model into a dependency graph and releases buildable steps — but it does not drive the robots in real time. Workers decide how to navigate, place, and verify on their own. The LEGO substrate is a modular assembly benchmark and low-cost research environment, not the commercial identity of the platform.

Primary goals

  • Develop a decentralized multi-agent assembly system.
  • Research swarm coordination under limited communication.
  • Validate stigmergic construction workflows against a centralized baseline.
  • Measure fault tolerance under partial robot failure.
  • Study scalability as robot count and structure complexity increase.
  • Establish a modular architecture capable of expansion toward construction-scale systems.

Secondary objectives

  • Develop generalized assembly-graph representations.
  • Evaluate distributed task-allocation strategies.
  • Explore autonomous construction sequencing.
  • Establish a simulation-to-reality (sim-to-real) robotics workflow.
  • Build reusable robotic research infrastructure.

Explicit non-goals

  • Real-time centralized control of robots by the Foreman.
  • Tall or out-of-reach structures beyond the 4-DOF arm envelope.
  • Generalized object manipulation beyond common LEGO geometries.
  • Outdoor, uneven, or uncontrolled-lighting environments.
  • Mechanical optimization beyond what research iteration requires.
  • Mass fabrication.
